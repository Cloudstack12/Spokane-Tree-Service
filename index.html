5 Reason To Have Your Tree Removed
In Spokane, Wa


Trees play a crucial role in our ecosystem and provide numerous benefits, such as improving air quality, conserving water, and enhancing the aesthetics of our surroundings. However, there are instances when tree removal becomes necessary, especially when it poses risks to our safety and property. In this article, we will explore five reasons why you may need to consider having a tree removed in Spokane.

From safeguarding your home against potential damage to ensuring the safety of your family and neighbors, tree removal can sometimes be the best option. Additionally, dealing with diseased or pest-infested trees, addressing overgrowth and encroachment issues, and enhancing the aesthetics and property value are all factors that can lead to the decision to remove a tree. As you read on, you’ll discover the importance of each reason and learn when it might be time to take action.

Protecting Your Home from Damage

To safeguard your home from potential harm, it’s crucial to consider removing trees that pose a risk to its structure or occupants. Trees with weak or damaged roots, those that lean dangerously close to your home, and those infested with pests or diseases can all pose a significant threat. Not only can these trees fall and cause extensive property damage or personal injury, but they can also disrupt your home’s foundation, damage your roof, or even break windows due to strong winds, storms, or heavy snowfall.

Removing such trees in a timely manner can help protect your home and family from potential harm. Spokane’s unique climate and weather conditions make it especially important to assess the health and stability of the trees on your property. By consulting with a professional tree removal service in Spokane, you can ensure that any problematic trees are identified and removed safely and efficiently, minimizing the risk to your home and loved ones.

Ensuring Safety of Family and Neighbors

Imagine the peace of mind you’ll gain knowing that your loved ones and neighbors are safe from the potential hazards of a dangerous tree looming over your property. Tree removal in Spokane is essential in ensuring the safety of your family and neighbors, especially if the tree is diseased, damaged, or structurally unsound. A tree with compromised integrity can easily fall or lose branches during storms or high winds, posing a significant risk to anyone in its vicinity. Moreover, falling trees or branches can cause severe injury or even fatality, so it is crucial to act promptly and have a professional remove any hazardous trees.

In addition to physical threats, hazardous trees can also cause damage to your neighbor’s property, potentially leading to disputes and strained relationships. By having your tree removed in Spokane, you are not only safeguarding the well-being of your family and neighbors, but also maintaining harmony within your community. Furthermore, local regulations may require you to remove a dangerous tree to ensure public safety. In these cases, acting responsibly and complying with the rules demonstrates your commitment to the welfare of those around you. So, do not hesitate to seek professional tree removal services in Spokane to guarantee the safety of your loved ones and neighbors.

Dealing with Diseased or Pest-Infested Trees

Don’t let diseased or pest-infested trees wreak havoc on your property and the surrounding environment; take action now and protect what matters most. Diseased trees can quickly decline in health, posing risks to other plants and trees in your yard. Pests, such as insects and rodents, can also infest your tree, leading to further damage and potential spread to other trees in the area. By having a professional remove a diseased or pest-infested tree, you will help prevent the spread of the issue and protect the health of your landscape.

In Spokane, there are several common tree diseases and pests to watch out for, such as Dutch Elm Disease, Black Knot, and the Emerald Ash Borer. If you suspect your tree is suffering from any of these issues, it is crucial to consult with a certified arborist or tree removal service. They can assess the situation, determine the best course of action, and safely remove the tree if necessary. Prompt action is essential to prevent further damage to your property and the local ecosystem, ensuring a healthy and thriving environment for everyone to enjoy.

Addressing Overgrowth and Encroachment Issues

It’s important to tackle overgrowth and encroachment issues before they get out of hand, as they can lead to property damage and safety hazards. Overgrown trees often have branches that extend beyond their intended space, potentially becoming entangled in power lines or causing damage to nearby structures. Furthermore, tree roots can spread beneath the surface, causing damage to sidewalks, driveways, and even the foundation of your home. Encroachment issues occur when a tree from one property extends onto another, which can lead to disputes between neighbors and even legal issues if not resolved.

In Spokane, addressing overgrowth and encroachment issues is crucial for maintaining a safe and harmonious environment. Regular tree maintenance, such as pruning and trimming, can help prevent these problems from arising in the first place. However, if a tree has already caused damage or poses a risk to your property, it may be necessary to have it removed. Professional tree removal services in Spokane can assess the situation and provide the most effective solution to protect your property and ensure the safety of everyone involved.

Enhancing Aesthetics and Property Value

By focusing on proper tree care and maintenance, you’re not only ensuring safety but also significantly enhancing the aesthetics and property value of your home. A well-manicured landscape with healthy, well-pruned trees can greatly improve the curb appeal and overall attractiveness of your property. On the other hand, trees that are diseased, dead, or overgrown can detract from your home’s appearance and lower its perceived value. By removing or properly maintaining these trees, you can create a more visually appealing outdoor space that will likely increase your property’s worth and appeal to potential buyers.

In addition to improving your home’s exterior appearance, proper tree care and removal can also positively impact your property’s overall health and sustainability. Removing dead or dying trees can prevent the spread of disease or pests to nearby healthy trees, thereby promoting a healthier ecosystem. Additionally, by carefully selecting and planting new trees more suited to your property’s conditions and environment, you can create a more sustainable and eco-friendly landscape that will be more attractive to potential buyers and improve your property’s long-term value.

Frequently Asked Questions


What is the average cost of tree removal in Spokane, and does it vary based on tree size or location?


The average cost of tree removal in Spokane typically ranges from $300 to $1,500, with the price varying based on factors such as tree size, location, and accessibility. Smaller trees may cost less to remove, while larger, more complex removals can lead to increased costs. Additionally, trees located in hard-to-reach areas, or those that pose a risk to nearby structures or utility lines, may result in higher fees due to the added challenges and equipment required for safe removal.


Are there any permits or regulations required to remove a tree in Spokane, and if so, how do I obtain them?


In Spokane, there may be permits and regulations required for tree removal depending on the specific circumstances. For trees located on private property, a permit is generally not needed unless the tree is protected under a development permit or is part of a landscape plan. However, for trees situated on public property, you must obtain permission from the City of Spokane. To do so, you can submit a tree removal request by contacting 311, using the 311 mobile app, or visiting the City of Spokane’s website. It is essential to follow these guidelines and obtain necessary permits to avoid fines or legal consequences.


How do I find a reputable and qualified tree removal service in the Spokane area?


Finding a reputable and qualified tree removal service in the Spokane area is essential for ensuring the safe and efficient removal of your tree. To locate a reliable company, start by asking for recommendations from friends, neighbors, or local landscaping businesses. Additionally, you can search online for companies with positive reviews and a strong presence in the local community. Be sure to verify that the tree removal service you choose is fully licensed, insured, and has a team of certified arborists on staff. Lastly, request multiple quotes from different companies to compare prices and services before making your final decision.


What is the recommended process for disposing of the removed tree and debris, and are there any recycling or repurposing options available?


After having a tree removed in Spokane, it is essential to properly dispose of the tree and debris. The recommended process involves chipping or shredding the tree branches and foliage to create mulch or wood chips, which can then be used for landscaping or gardening purposes. For larger pieces of wood, consider cutting them into firewood or donating them to local organizations that repurpose wood for various projects. Additionally, certain facilities in Spokane accept tree debris for recycling, where they may be processed into compost or other environmentally-friendly products. Always check with your local waste management or recycling center for specific guidelines and requirements on tree debris disposal.


How can I determine if a tree on my property is at risk of causing damage or becoming a safety issue, and what proactive measures can be taken to prevent these issues?


To determine if a tree on your property is at risk of causing damage or becoming a safety issue, it is essential to regularly inspect it for signs of decay, disease, or structural instability. Look for dead or dying branches, cracks in the trunk, fungal growths, or signs of insect infestations. Additionally, consider the tree’s proximity to structures, power lines, or areas with high foot traffic. To proactively prevent issues, schedule regular maintenance and pruning to encourage healthy growth, and consult with an arborist or tree care professional if you suspect any problems. They can help to identify potential hazards and recommend appropriate preventative measures, such as bracing, cabling, or targeted pruning, to ensure the tree remains stable and safe for the surrounding environment.

Plus, let’s not forget that a well-maintained yard can totally boost your property’s value and curb appeal. So if you’re on the fence about removing that tree, maybe it’s time to take the plunge and give your space a fresh new look.
